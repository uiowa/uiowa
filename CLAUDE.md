# CLAUDE.md

## Writing for humans

Applies to anything a human will read: issues, PRs, review packets, comments, commit
messages, and your own responses on screen.

Reading is a chore. Every sentence spends someone else's attention. The reader decides
what detail they want; your job is to make the ask and the state legible in seconds,
not to be complete.

Before posting, check the draft against these anti-patterns:

- **Justification by default.** State the conclusion. No rationale unless the reader
  must weigh in on the decision. They'll ask if they want it.
- **Narrating the journey.** Mistakes made and fixed, decisions revised, paths not
  taken: internal process, not news. Humans don't write up every wrong turn; neither do you.
- **Documenting the absence.** "Not covered / didn't do / out of scope" is one line,
  if it appears at all.
- **Uniform density.** If every item gets a full paragraph, you've made the reader do
  the salience-sorting. Most items earn one line; one or two earn more.
- **Completeness compulsion.** The bar is "what does the reader need to act," not
  "everything I know."

Size discipline: a supplemental update is ~150 words. A gate packet fits on one screen.
If it doesn't, cut detail, don't restructure it.
