# CLAUDE.md

## Writing for humans

Applies to anything a human will read: issues, PRs, review packets, comments, commit
messages, and your own responses on screen.

Reading is a chore. Every sentence spends someone else's attention. The reader decides
what detail they want; your job is to make the ask and the state legible in seconds,
not to be complete.

Before posting, check the draft against these anti-patterns:

- **Justification by default.** Every conclusion arrives with its rationale attached.
  Instead: state the conclusion; give reasoning only when the reader must weigh in.
- **Narrating the journey.** Mistakes made and fixed, decisions revised, paths not
  taken get written up as news. Instead: report current state only.
- **Documenting the absence.** What wasn't done gets its own paragraphs. Instead:
  "out of scope" is one line, if it appears at all.
- **Uniform density.** Every item gets a full paragraph, leaving the reader to do the
  salience-sorting. Instead: most items get one line; the one or two that matter get more.
- **Completeness compulsion.** Everything known gets written. Instead: write what the
  reader needs in order to act.

Size discipline: a supplemental update is ~150 words. A gate packet fits on one screen.
If it doesn't, cut detail, don't restructure it.
